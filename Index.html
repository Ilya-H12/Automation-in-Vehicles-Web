Report summary

Key findings

1 · Topic description

Automated vehicles use cameras, radar, LiDAR, and AI to perceive the environment, plan movement, and assist the driver. Most systems today are Level 1–2 assistance.

Characteristics

  • Continuous environment mapping
  • Real-time decision-making
  • Over-the-air updates

2 · Cost & economics

Higher automation adds $4k–7k to manufacturing cost, translating to $10k–18k for consumers. Costs will drop as components scale.

Barriers

  • Regulation & liability
  • Human misuse
  • Cybersecurity
3 · Future outlook

Where automation is heading

Level 2+ will become standard in the 2030s. Highway automation and geo-fenced robotaxis will expand, reshaping mobility, logistics, and insurance models.
